Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

What is the crime? UPI payment fraud is a widespread cybercrime in India. In this scam, criminals trick individuals into approving fake digital payment requests or sharing sensitive banking details, like OTPs and UPI PINs, to steal their money.

How it happens :

A college student lists a used laptop for sale on an online marketplace. A fraudster contacts them, pretending to be a buyer eager to pay immediately. Instead of transferring funds, the scammer sends a UPI "collect request" to the student's payment app. The scammer falsely instructs the student that approving this request and entering their secret UPI PIN will successfully deposit the money. The confused student enters their PIN. Because they approved a collect request, the money is instantly deducted from their own bank account instead.

Who is targeted? Scammers frequently target college students, first-time online sellers, and people who are unfamiliar with the exact mechanics of digital payment applications.

What are the consequences? The primary consequence is immediate financial loss. For a student, losing their limited savings causes immense mental stress. Furthermore, it is incredibly difficult to recover stolen funds. Always remember: a UPI PIN is only used to send money, never to receive it.
